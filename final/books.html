books

books are more than just stories—they’re entire worlds, ideas wrapped in words, echoes of minds from centuries past. some leave a mark, others shift the way i think, and a few feel like home. this is a collection of what i’ve read—the books that challenged me, inspired me, or simply refused to be forgotten.



a song to drown rivers - ann liang

★ ★ ★ ★ ☆

a journey through love’s depths and the quiet sorrow that comes with growing up. where magic stirs and hearts break, leaving you with a quiet ache that stays long after the last page.


the stolen heir - holly black

★ ★ ★ ★ ☆

a return to the fae realm, where betrayal and desire twist into something sharp. a story of power and escape, laced with danger, but not quite as unpredictable as one might hope.


divine rivals - rebecca ross

★ ★ ★ ★ ☆

a tale of forbidden love woven through a war-torn world, where magic binds two souls together in a destiny they can’t escape. captivating, yet held back by moments of quiet pacing.


the assasin's blade - sarah j mass

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

before she became a legend, she was just a girl with a dagger and a broken heart. fast, fierce, and filled with shadows—this is where it all began.


we - yevgeny zamyatin

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

a dystopian world where freedom is erased, and conformity is law. chilling, fragmented, and eerily prescient, it lays bare the price of peace in a world where individuality is a crime.


heartless - marissa meyer

★ ★ ★ ☆ ☆

a prequel to wonderland. before the queen was cruel, she was just a girl with dreams too big for a world that crushed them. a tragic descent into madness, but it’s the slow unraveling that makes it all the more heart-wrenching.
